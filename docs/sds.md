# Student Disability Services — Accommodation Policies

> **Please read this page in full before emailing me about accommodations.** It answers the most common questions and applies to all of my courses. If your question is already answered here or in the course syllabus, I will refer you back to this page.

**Note on scope.** This document covers policies across all of my courses. Not every course includes every component described here — for example, some courses have no exams, and only some include debates, presentations, or other in-class activities. Where a section applies only to certain courses, this is noted explicitly.

## Classroom Access Accommodations

### Audio or Video Recording of Lectures

I do not consent to audio or video recording of complete lectures. As an alternative, I maintain a set of recorded lectures on YouTube that are linked from every course website; these cover the same material and are available at any time. In addition:

- You may take photographs of slides and whiteboard content (see below).
- You may use a computer or tablet for note-taking during class.
- Office hours are available for clarification on any lecture content.

### Computer Use for Note-Taking

Permitted. You may use a laptop or tablet for note-taking during class. Please position your screen so that it is not distracting to students seated near you.

### Photographing Visual Content

Permitted. You may photograph slides, whiteboard content, and other visual materials shown in class. These materials are for personal study use only and may not be shared publicly.

## Assignment Accommodations

### Deadline Extensions and Attendance/Deadline Flexibility

**The 96-hour flexible grace period is my implementation of deadline-extension and attendance/deadline-flexibility accommodations.** No additional individual deadline accommodations are provided beyond this policy, because the policy already incorporates substantial flexibility for all students.

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Grace period | 96 hours of flexible late time, usable across all assignments in the quarter |
| Who receives it | Every student, including those with SDS deadline-extension or attendance/flexibility accommodations |
| How time is counted | Every hour late (or fraction thereof) counts against your balance |
| After the grace period is exhausted | Late work receives half credit if submitted within one week |
| Assignment completion | You must still complete all assignments to pass the course |

If you have an SDS "attendance and deadline flexibility" accommodation — including the Harris Flexibility Agreement — the 96-hour grace period serves as the flexibility mechanism for this course. No separate flexibility agreement is required.

### Attendance and Participation

I do not take formal attendance in my courses. Participation is a component of the final grade and is implemented as follows:

- **In-class quizzes** are part of the participation grade. There are no make-up quizzes; your grade is based only on quizzes actually taken.
- **Presentations, debates, or other scheduled in-class activities** (where applicable to the course) are essential learning requirements. Students sign up for the date on which they wish to participate, so you may select a date that works with your schedule and any accommodation needs.

### Spelling and Grammar Leniency

This accommodation is not applicable in my courses. Spell check and grammar check are universally available in the tools students use to prepare typed submissions, and grading focuses on mastery of course concepts rather than surface-level errors. Written work is expected to communicate ideas clearly and professionally, with the understanding that occasional spelling or grammar errors will occur and are not treated as a substantive part of the grade. The same principle applies on exams — which are multiple choice or short answer — with one exception: when a specific technical term is itself the answer being assessed, correctly identifying (including spelling) that term is part of what is being tested.

## Exam Accommodations — Where Applicable

> **This section applies only to courses that include exams.** Some of my courses have no exams at all; in those courses, the policies below are moot. Please consult the course syllabus first.

### Extra Time (1.25x, 1.5x, 2.0x)

Not applicable. All exams in my courses are **untimed** — no time limits are imposed. Extra-time accommodations are therefore already subsumed by the standard testing format.

### Reduced-Distraction Testing Environment

The standard exam environment is designed to be quiet and distraction-free. If you require a separate room as part of your accommodation:

- You may take the exam **one day early** in my office or a TA's office.
- The exam must be taken in the presence of a TA or the instructor.
- Rescheduled exams may include an additional oral component, as appropriate.
- You must request alternate exam scheduling **at least two weeks in advance** of the exam date.

### Computer Use During Exams

Not implementable. All exams are multiple choice or very short answer format. Use of computers or typed responses is not permitted during exams; this format is an essential requirement of the course assessment structure.

### SDS Proctoring and the Alternative Testing Agreement

I do not use SDS proctoring for my exams and do not complete the Alternative Testing Agreement (ATA). Exams are administered by me or by a TA, either in class or — for students with a reduced-distraction accommodation — in my office or a TA's office as described above.
