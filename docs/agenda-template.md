# Class Meeting Agenda Template (80-minute)

This is the standard-term agenda template. For the 3-hour summer format, see
[agenda-template-summer.md](agenda-template-summer.md).

Each 80-minute class meeting is built around four blocks: **lecture**,
**reading response discussion**, **student presentation**, and **hands-on
activity or breakout discussion**. Because 80 minutes is tight, we typically
pick **one** of hands-on activity or breakout discussion per meeting rather
than both — see the [breakouts index](breakouts/) and the [activities
index](activities/) to plan the mix week-to-week.

Two motifs work well. Pick whichever fits the day's material.

## Motif A — Discussion sandwich

Best when the assigned reading is dense and student-led discussion should
carry the room's energy into the lecture.

| Time      | Min | Block                                                | Notes                                   |
|-----------|-----|------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| 0:00–0:05 |   5 | Housekeeping + warm-up prompt                        |                                         |
| 0:05–0:15 |  10 | Reading response Q&A                                 | Instructor facilitates                  |
| 0:15–0:35 |  20 | Student presentation                                 | Student-led                             |
| 0:35–0:55 |  20 | Lecture                                              | Book concepts and technical framing     |
| 0:55–1:15 |  20 | Breakout **or** hands-on activity                    | Small groups; report-back at the end    |
| 1:15–1:20 |   5 | Wrap + reading preview                               |                                         |

## Motif B — Frontloaded lecture

Best when the day introduces new technical machinery students need before
they can meaningfully discuss.

| Time      | Min | Block                                                | Notes                                   |
|-----------|-----|------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| 0:00–0:10 |  10 | Housekeeping + reading Q&A quick hits                |                                         |
| 0:10–0:35 |  25 | Lecture                                              | Book concepts and technical framing     |
| 0:35–0:55 |  20 | Student presentation                                 | Student-led                             |
| 0:55–1:15 |  20 | Hands-on activity **or** breakout                    | Small groups; report-back at the end    |
| 1:15–1:20 |   5 | Wrap + reading preview                               |                                         |

## Notes on adaptation

- **Hands-on vs. breakout.** Alternate week-to-week. Weeks with a substantial
  lab (DNS, BGP, throttling detection, OONI) lean hands-on; weeks whose
  material is more policy- or platform-oriented lean breakout.
- **Reading Q&A.** Students post reading responses ahead of class; use the
  Q&A slot to work through the most common questions or points of confusion.
- **Student presentations.** ~20 minutes of material; leave room for
  audience Q&A after.
- **Breakouts.** See `docs/breakouts/<topic>.md` for two debate topics per
  lecture; groups pick one and report back.
