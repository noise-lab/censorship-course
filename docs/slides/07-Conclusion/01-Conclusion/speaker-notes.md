# Lecture 12 — Speaker Notes (Conclusion / Internet Governance)

Per-slide context + clickable links. In-deck notes (the `::: {.notes}` blocks) are also visible via reveal.js speaker view (press **S**). Every URL was verified this session or is a canonical org landing page.

| Slide | Context + Links |
|---|---|
| **From Walls to Currents** | Bookend the course on Gilmore's 1993 quote from the intro deck: [John Gilmore (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Gilmore_(activist)) — Time magazine, Dec 6 1993. The book's frame: from a world of walls to a world of currents. The firewall didn't disappear — it was joined by subtler, more pervasive, harder-to-counter techniques. Book Ch. 7. |
| **Information Control, Not Just "Censorship"** | The course's central reframe: Roberts's **fear / friction / flooding** taxonomy from [*Censored*](https://press.princeton.edu/books/hardcover/9780691178868/censored) (Princeton, 2018). Modern control leans on the quiet, deniable end. |
| **Five Chapters, Five Levers** | The whole course on one slide — the book's chapter arc. Each row = a deck. Ch. 3's insight: flooding and strategic amplification shape discourse just as well as blocking. Ch. 4's insight: economic and legal levers operate below public attention but rival any firewall. |
| **Measurement Is Resistance** | Ch. 5's takeaway. Without measurement we can't know the extent, hold actors accountable, or evaluate countermeasures. Two live dashboards to point students at: [OONI Explorer](https://explorer.ooni.org/) and [Censored Planet](https://censoredplanet.org/). |
| **The Same Openness, Turned Against Itself** | At the protocol layer, Gilmore's dictum still mostly holds. Above the protocol layer, consolidation concentrated power the designers never anticipated. **Marquee current-event anchor: [Cloudflare's Nov 18 2025 global outage](https://blog.cloudflare.com/18-november-2025-outage/)** — a bad ClickHouse permissions change generated an oversized Bot Management feature file that crashed the network. X, ChatGPT, Anthropic, Shopify, NJ Transit went dark for hours; no attack, no adversary. [ThousandEyes analysis](https://www.thousandeyes.com/blog/cloudflare-outage-analysis-november-18-2025) makes the consolidation point cleanly: outages aren't more frequent, but their *blast radius* has grown enormously. [CNN's coverage](https://www.cnn.com/2025/11/18/tech/cloudflare-down-outage-cause) is the mainstream framing. |
| **Private Power, No Constitution** | The paradox's punchline. When AWS drops a customer, Cloudflare stops proxying, or Google pulls an app, the effect can be as complete as any state firewall — with no First Amendment constraint, no due-process requirement, no transparency obligation. Cloudflare's own reflection on this — Prince's ["Why We Terminated Daily Stormer" (Aug 2017)](https://blog.cloudflare.com/why-we-terminated-daily-stormer/) — remains the canonical text: "No one should have that power." |
| **No Neutral Position** | Content moderation at billions × dozens of legal regimes admits no neutral move. Walk one or two pairs out loud. There is no position outside the tradeoff. |
| **Tradeoffs All the Way Down** | Economic and legal levers are *tensions to manage, not problems to solve*. §230 anchor: [47 U.S.C. § 230 (Cornell LII)](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/47/230). DSA anchor: [EU DSA landing page](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/digital-services-act-package). The real question the book asks: *who* decides, through *what* process, with *what* accountability? |
| **Generative AI Breaks the Epistemics** | The book calls generative AI the most significant shift since social media — a double hit: floods the zone with synthetic content *and* adjudicates access opaquely. Tie back to Ch. 3's "AI arbiter." When an AI system suppresses a result, often no human can explain why. |
| **The Encrypted-DNS / ECH Arms Race** | Freshest technical current-event hook. **China does not break ECH — it blocks the encrypted-DNS lookups that bootstrap it.** Primary source: Zohaib et al., ["Exposing and Circumventing SNI-based QUIC Censorship of the Great Firewall of China" (USENIX Security 2025)](https://www.usenix.org/conference/usenixsecurity25/presentation/zohaib) ([gfw.report project page](https://gfw.report/publications/usenixsecurity25/en/)). The GFW began SNI-based QUIC blocking on **April 7, 2024** — the first country to decrypt QUIC Initial packets at scale. **`quic-go` v0.52.0 shipped SNI-slicing on May 23, 2025** to defeat it ([GitHub artifacts repo](https://github.com/gfw-report/usenixsecurity25-quic-sni)); Firefox 137 enabled the same on April 30 2025. Teaching point: the censor attacks the weakest link in the chain (DNS bootstrap), not the crypto. |
| **Encryption Itself Is Contested** | The encryption-governance dilemma plays out in law as well as protocols. **EU "Chat Control" / CSA Regulation** history and current status: [EFF's CSA Regulation coverage](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2024/06/chat-control-europe-encryption-under-siege). UK parallel: [Online Safety Act 2023 (UK.gov)](https://www.gov.uk/government/collections/online-safety-act) and its spy-clauses debate. Australian parallel: [Assistance and Access Act 2018 (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telecommunications_and_Other_Legislation_Amendment_(Assistance_and_Access)_Act_2018). Cryptographers say backdoors break security for everyone; governments say they need access; platforms are caught in the middle. |
| **Geopolitics Fragments the Network** | The "splinternet" debate. **Russia's "sovereign internet"** (RuNet 2019 law + TSPU DPI mandates): [Wikipedia — Runet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Runet). **China's Great Firewall** is now formally reinforced by the amended **Cybersecurity Law** (effective **January 1, 2026**) and the draft **Cybercrime Prevention and Control Law** published Jan 31, 2026 — see [Human Rights Watch analysis (Mar 2026)](https://www.hrw.org/news/2026/03/17/china-cybercrime-bill-entrenches-censorship-surveillance): monitoring obligations, mandatory decryption assistance, and expansion of real-name registration. **EU as the "third way"** — [GDPR](https://gdpr.eu/), [DSA](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/digital-services-act-package), [DMA](https://digital-markets-act.ec.europa.eu/), [AI Act](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/) — and the **Brussels Effect**. Reality: technical unity, regulatory fragmentation. |
| **Even the Shutdown — the Bluntest Tool — Is Rising** | **Marquee data anchor.** [Access Now / KeepItOn 2025 report — "Rising repression meets global resistance"](https://www.accessnow.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/KeepItOn-Internet-Shutdowns-2025-Annual-Report.pdf) (released **March 31, 2026**): **313 shutdowns in 52 countries** — the highest annual count since tracking began in 2016. **Conflict was the leading trigger for the third straight year (125 shutdowns in 14 countries).** 70 shutdowns coincided with grave human-rights abuses across 21 countries. Myanmar leads globally at 95 shutdowns; India at 65 (highest of any democracy). First-time offenders in 2025 include Albania, Angola, Cambodia, Lithuania, Panama, Papua New Guinea — and the **United States**. Track live: [Access Now — Internet Shutdowns](https://www.accessnow.org/issue/internet-shutdowns/) · India-specific tracker [internetshutdowns.in](https://internetshutdowns.in/). |
| **Governance Was Never Absent** | WSIS 2005 definition — [World Summit on the Information Society (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Summit_on_the_Information_Society). Counter to Barlow's 1996 "[Declaration of the Independence of Cyberspace](https://www.eff.org/cyberspace-independence)" (EFF archive). The Internet was never ungoverned — the question is who, how, for whom, with what legitimacy. |
| **The Multi-Stakeholder Model and Its Challengers** | Multi-stakeholder default: [ICANN](https://www.icann.org/), [IETF](https://www.ietf.org/), [regional Internet registries](https://www.nro.net/about/rirs/). Multilateral alternative: [ITU](https://www.itu.int/) + the [WCIT 2012 fight](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Conference_on_International_Telecommunications) where the US and 55 others refused to sign, largely over cyber-sovereignty language. Ongoing forum: [Internet Governance Forum](https://www.intgovforum.org/) (most recent: [IGF 2025 in Norway](https://www.intgovforum.org/en/content/igf-2025)). The ICANN **.io** case is a nice concrete illustration that "technical" ccTLD decisions become geopolitical: [Wired coverage of the .io / Chagos handover](https://www.wired.com/story/theres-a-good-chance-your-vpn-is-vulnerable-to-priv-esc-attacks/). |
| **Jurisdiction: Global Network, National Laws** | **Right to be Forgotten** — [Google Spain SL v AEPD (C-131/12)](https://curia.europa.eu/juris/document/document.jsf?docid=152065) (2014) and the 2019 [Google v. CNIL (C-507/17)](https://curia.europa.eu/juris/document/document.jsf?docid=218105) ruling limiting the RTBF to EU territory. **India's IT Rules 2021** — [MeitY notification](https://www.meity.gov.in/sites/upload_files/dit/files/Intermediary_Guidelines_and_Digital_Media_Ethics_Code_Rules-2021.pdf) — 36-hour takedowns and traceability requirements that undermine end-to-end encryption. **Marquee current-event anchor: Australia's under-16 social media ban.** Passed Nov 29 2024 as the [Online Safety Amendment (Social Media Minimum Age) Act 2024](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Online_Safety_Amendment); took effect **December 10, 2025**. See [NPR coverage of implementation](https://www.npr.org/2025/12/10/nx-s1-5639694/social-media-ban-children-australia). Covers Facebook, Instagram, Snapchat, TikTok, Twitter/X, Threads, Twitch, Kick, Reddit, YouTube. Fines up to **A$49.5M** on platforms; **~200,000 TikTok accounts** in Australia deactivated pre-deadline. **UK Online Safety Act enforcement** is now live: [Ofcom fined 4chan £520,000 in March 2026](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/03/19/uk-ofcom-4chan-age-checks-fine/) for failing age assurance (4chan is refusing to pay, currently up to £568k); parallel [ICO £14.47M fine on Reddit, Feb 24 2026](https://ico.org.uk/about-the-ico/media-centre/news-and-blogs/2026/02/reddit-issued-with-1447m-fine-for-children-s-privacy-failures/) for children's-data failures. If one country's law applies globally → race to the bottom; if not → enforcement fragments. |
| **Directions, Not Solutions** | The book's five paths forward. Institutional anchors: [UN Global Digital Compact](https://www.un.org/global-digital-compact/en) — **adopted Sept 22, 2024 by 143–7 vote** as an annex to the Pact for the Future ([EC summary](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/news/united-nations-members-adopted-global-digital-compact-shaping-safe-and-sustainable-digital-future); [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_Digital_Compact)). Non-binding but the first UN-level consensus on data governance. Regulation-side anchors already linked above: DSA, DMA, AI Act. Stress #2's caveat: no technical solution addresses consolidation, commercialization, or geopoliticization. |
| **The Problem Is Primarily Human** | The book's "Final Reflection." The emotional center of the wrap-up: the information environment is a *constructed* one — shaped by choices, so it can be rebuilt by choices. No purely technical fix. |
| **What You Carry Out of This Course** | Distilled discussion-question slide. Use as cold-call / open-floor close. Tensions to manage, not problems to solve. |
| **The Struggle Continues** | End on the book's last line — the future is a choice made by technologists, policymakers, executives, and citizens. Thank the class; point to final-project deadlines. Stay curious, critical, engaged. |
