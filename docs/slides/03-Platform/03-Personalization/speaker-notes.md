# Lecture 6 — Speaker Notes (Personalization / Filter Bubbles)

Per-slide context + clickable links. In-deck notes (the `::: {.notes}` blocks) are also visible via reveal.js speaker view (press **S**). Every URL was verified this session or is a canonical org landing page.

| Slide | Context + Links |
|---|---|
| **"A Squirrel Dying in Front of Your House"** | Attributed to Mark Zuckerberg in Eli Pariser, [*The Filter Bubble: What the Internet Is Hiding From You*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Filter_Bubble) (Penguin, 2011). Line originally reported by David Kirkpatrick in [*The Facebook Effect*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Facebook_Effect) (Simon & Schuster, 2010). Captures the engagement logic of personalization: the feed optimizes for what holds *your* attention, not for what you ought to see. Book §3.5. |
| **Not All Control Is Overt** | Tie back to the Roberts taxonomy from Lecture 1: fear / friction / flooding. Personalization is friction (content is *harder* to reach because it's buried or never surfaced) and it sets up flooding (data voids, later). Key analytic claim from the book: personalization is information control *because* the user can't observe what was filtered. No backlash, no martyr, full deniability — "it's just the algorithm." Roberts, [*Censored*](https://press.princeton.edu/books/hardcover/9780691178868/censored), Ch. 2. |
| **Personalization Is Ubiquitous** | Book §3.5. The same mechanism spans every content type. Most of it feels benign or even helpful. That's the wedge: the *same* machinery that gives you the nearest store can give you a different reality on "Egypt," "vaccines," or a ballot measure. Foundational academic frame: Hannak et al., ["Measuring Personalization of Web Search"](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/2488388.2488435) (WWW 2013) — the empirical baseline for how much Google personalizes. |
| **From Benign to Political** | Core conceptual slide. Personalization sits on a continuum with censorship; there's no bright line. The censor doesn't have to remove content — making it un-surface for the people who'd care is enough. *User's lack of awareness* is what makes it powerful. Reference: Sunstein, [*#Republic: Divided Democracy in the Age of Social Media*](https://press.princeton.edu/books/hardcover/9780691175515/republic) (Princeton, 2017) — updates the "Daily Me" thesis for the algorithmic feed. |
| **Filter Bubbles and Echo Chambers** | Pariser (2011) is the canonical popularization. Be honest that the strong 2011 thesis has been partly walked back. Interaction of algorithmic curation and human **selective exposure** is what recent work emphasizes. Key nuanced treatments: Bail et al., ["Exposure to opposing views on social media can increase political polarization"](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1804840115) (PNAS 2018); Guess et al., ["Reshares on social media amplify political news but do not detectably affect beliefs or opinions"](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.abp9364) (*Science*, 2023, part of the [Meta 2020 US Election Studies](https://research.facebook.com/2020-election-research/)); Systematic review: Ahmmad et al., ["Trap of Social Media Algorithms"](https://www.mdpi.com/2075-4698/15/11/301) (*Societies* 15:301, 2025). Field converges: bubbles are a product of algorithms *and* human choice, not either alone. |
| **The Bubble Has Moved: Passive Signals** | **TikTok "For You" curates on passive signals:** [Wall Street Journal, "Inside TikTok's Algorithm"](https://www.wsj.com/video/series/inside-tiktoks-highly-secretive-algorithm/investigation-how-tiktok-algorithm-figures-out-your-deepest-desires/6C0C2040-FF25-4827-8528-2BD6612E3796) (2021 sock-puppet audit — 100 bot accounts, watch-time drives everything). Also the leaked ["TikTok Culture" internal note](https://www.nytimes.com/2021/12/06/business/media/tiktok-algorithm.html) (NYT, Dec 2021). **Marquee current-event case: [Ibrahim et al., "Systematic partisan content skews in TikTok during the 2024 US elections," *Nature* (2026)](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10447-1)** — NYU Abu Dhabi researchers ran 323 audit experiments with sock-puppet accounts in NY, TX, GA. Across 280,000+ recommendations over 27 weeks, Republican-seeded accounts got ~11.5% more co-partisan content; Democratic-seeded accounts got ~7.5% more cross-partisan content. Asymmetries can't be explained by engagement metrics. TikTok's bot-detection cut off data collection Nov 11, 2024. Preprint: [arXiv:2501.17831](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.17831). Coverage: [phys.org](https://phys.org/news/2026-05-tiktok-algorithm-pro-republican-bias.html). **Closed-platform bubbles (WhatsApp / Telegram):** see [Reis et al., "Can WhatsApp Counter Misinformation?"](https://arxiv.org/abs/1911.05147) and coverage of [Brazil/India WhatsApp election misinformation](https://www.nytimes.com/2018/10/17/world/americas/whatsapp-brazil-presidential-election.html). |
| **Weak Signals, Strong Inferences** | **Narayanan, ["Understanding Social Media Recommendation Algorithms"](https://knightcolumbia.org/content/understanding-social-media-recommendation-algorithms)** (Knight First Amendment Institute, Mar 2023) — the go-to primer on how engagement-optimized systems recruit *any* signal correlated with watch time. **Ribeiro et al., ["Auditing Radicalization Pathways on YouTube"](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3351095.3372879)** (FAccT 2020) — mainstream → IDW → alt-lite → alt-right migration. Contested: Ledwich & Zaitsev (2019) found the opposite with anonymous seeds — mechanism strength depends on how much personalization signal the system already has. The Star Wars / *The Last Jedi* fandom survey is an illustration, not a peer-reviewed finding; do not overclaim from it. |
| **The "Rabbit Hole": Contested Evidence** | Teaching goal here is epistemic humility, not a verdict — the strong "YouTube radicalizes people" claim (popular ~2018–19, e.g. Zeynep Tufekci's "rabbit hole" framing) has been substantially complicated. **Evidence for a pull:** Haroon et al., "Auditing YouTube's Recommendation System for Ideologically Congenial, Extreme, and Problematic Recommendations" (*PNAS*, 2023) — a 100,000-sock-puppet audit found recommendations grow *more congenial* and *more problematic* deeper in the trail, but did **not** find meaningful increases in ideological *extremity*. **Evidence against:** naturalistic studies (e.g. Hosseinmardi et al., *PNAS* 2021/2024) find limited polarization effects and a strong role for subscriptions and off-platform choices — "rabbit holes" driven by user choice more than the feed alone. (PNAS DOIs `10.1073/pnas.2213020120` and `10.1073/pnas.2318127122` are cited in the deck but returned 403 when checked this session, so no direct link here.) The honest read: **congenial, yes; an automatic extremism escalator, not shown.** Why so mixed? The algorithm changes constantly and outsiders can't see it — the same measurement problem as the DSA slide next. Don't let students leave thinking the science is settled either way. |
| **2025: Regulators Demand the Algorithm Open Up** | **Marquee current-event case: European Commission, Oct 24, 2025** — preliminary findings that TikTok and Meta breach [DSA Article 40](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/digital-services-act-package) for failing to give vetted researchers meaningful access to platform data. Fines up to 6% of global annual turnover — ~$9.87B for Meta, ~$1.38B for TikTok. Delegated act on researcher data access took effect Oct 29, 2025. Sources: [ASIL summary](https://www.asil.org/ILIB/european-commission-preliminarily-finds-tiktok-and-meta-breach-digital-services-act) · [MediaLaws](https://www.medialaws.eu/tiktok-and-meta-in-the-spotlight-for-alleged-dsa-breaches/) · [ComplianceHub](https://www.compliancehub.wiki/europe-flexes-its-regulatory-muscle-meta-and-tiktok-face-historic-dsa-enforcement-action/). **Feb 6, 2026 update:** the Commission also issued preliminary findings against TikTok specifically for **addictive design** — infinite scroll, autoplay, push notifications, and the personalized recommender itself (first DSA action targeting design rather than illegal content), with the same up-to-6%-of-turnover exposure. Primary source: [European Commission — Commission preliminarily finds TikTok's addictive design in breach of the DSA (Feb 2026)](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/news/commission-preliminarily-finds-tiktoks-addictive-design-breach-digital-services-act); background: [European Parliament EPRS analysis](https://epthinktank.eu/2026/05/06/addictive-design-on-online-platforms/). **Fresh empirical anchor (Jan 2026):** Bekavac & Mayer, [arXiv — "Auditing Meta and TikTok Research API Data Access under Article 40(12) of the DSA" (Jan 2026)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.12390) quantifies exactly why the Oct 2025 finding bites: the research APIs exclude up to ~50% of publicly visible activity, strip up to ~83% of contextual metadata, and cap researchers near ~1,000 requests/day — concrete evidence that outsiders still can't measure the bubble even with the DSA in force. Companion regulatory anchor: [DSA Article 38](https://dsa-library.com/article/38/) requires VLOPs/VLOSEs to offer at least one recommender-system option **not based on profiling** (chronological or similar). Article 27 covers general recommender-system transparency for all platforms. The fight is now explicitly about **algorithmic transparency** — can outsiders even *measure* the bubble? |
| **Auditing Recommendation Systems Is Hard** | Frame measurement as an adversarial, methods-heavy problem — the same flavor as Ch. 5. General audit-methods primer: Sandvig et al., ["Auditing Algorithms"](https://social.cs.uiuc.edu/papers/pdfs/ICA2014-Sandvig.pdf) (ICA 2014). Data-donation exemplar: **Mozilla RegretsReporter** ([research page](https://foundation.mozilla.org/en/blog/mozilla-investigation-youtubes-recommender-ai-still-a-horrorshow/), 2021) — 37,380 volunteers reported YouTube "regrets." The DSA fight is precisely about whether sanctioned data access can replace the sock-puppet / data-donation improv. |
| **Bobble: Seeing What Was Filtered** | Xing, Meng, Doozan, Feamster, Lee & Snoeren, ["Take This Personally: Pollution Attacks on Personalized Services"](https://www.usenix.org/conference/usenixsecurity13/technical-sessions/presentation/xing) (USENIX Security 2013) — the pollution-attack paper. Companion Bobble measurement paper: Xing et al., ["Exposing Inconsistent Web Search Results with Bobble"](https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-319-04918-2_13) (PAM 2014). Design: execute the query from many vantage points, then diff the result sets. UI surfaces results filtered from *this* user but visible elsewhere. |
| **What Causes the Differences?** | The study (~200 users, 75,000+ queries, nine months in 2012) tried to attribute inconsistencies to causes. Two big buckets: *who you are* (profile) and *where you are* (geography). Establishing causality is genuinely hard — these factors covary. Caveat the students: 2012-era data; specific numbers likely look different today, but the method and phenomenon persist. |
| **Bobble Findings: Geography Dominates** | Headline (Xing et al., PAM 2014): >85% of queries had a result absent from the user's set but in the top 10 for some other geography; geography > profile as an explanatory factor; logged-in vs. logged-out often changed the *order* more than the *set*. So "censorship by ranking" doesn't require removing anything. Durable lesson is the framing, not the 2012 percentages. |
| **Adversarial Personalization** | Xing et al., ["Take This Personally"](https://www.usenix.org/conference/usenixsecurity13/technical-sessions/presentation/xing) (USENIX Security 2013). Key escalation: personalization isn't just a passive side effect — it's an *attack surface*. The corpus is untouched; the attacker manipulates the *profile* so a target sees different results. Information control without takedowns — perfectly deniable. Recent parallel worth naming: [Aggarwal et al., "Search-engine-optimized SEO poisoning attacks"](https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.13731) and the broader search-manipulation literature. |
| **Data Voids** | Golebiewski & boyd, ["Data Voids: Where Missing Data Can Easily Be Exploited"](https://datasociety.net/library/data-voids/) (Data & Society, 2018/2019). Data voids are the flip side of the bubble: instead of burying content, you *plant* it where nothing trustworthy exists yet. Coin a term ("crisis actor," an invented syndrome), then own the search results for it. Recent measurement: Nozza et al. and follow-on arXiv work on Google warning banners find Google surfaces a warning banner on ~1% of queries — voids largely go unflagged. Ties to the Pravda / LLM-grooming debate we covered in Lecture 5 (Kertysova et al. 2025 argues chatbots reproduce disinformation *into voids* rather than because of successful grooming). |
| **Chatbots: The New Surface for Voids** | Frame chatbots as the *newest* curation surface: instead of ranking ten links, the model returns one synthesized answer — so the void problem gets worse, because there's no result list to inspect and no obvious "I didn't see X." **Marquee current-event case:** NewsGuard, [NewsGuard — "A Well-Funded Moscow-Based Global 'News' Network Has Infected Western AI Tools Worldwide With Russian Propaganda" (Mar 2025)](https://www.newsguardtech.com/special-reports/moscow-based-global-news-network-infected-western-artificial-intelligence-russian-propaganda/) audited **10 leading chatbots** (ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot, Grok, Perplexity, Meta AI, Mistral, others) and found they repeated false claims seeded by the pro-Kremlin **"Pravda" network ~33% of the time**; the seeded sites exist largely to be *scraped* by models, not read by humans — void-filling aimed at the training/retrieval pipeline. NewsGuard's follow-on 2026 audits find the pattern persists (and, in some audits, worsens for individual models). **The nuance — and why the slide's vignette is a *question*:** a *Harvard Kennedy School Misinformation Review* (2025) reanalysis argues the chatbots surface these sources mainly on **narrow/obscure prompts**, consistent with **data voids** rather than deliberate "LLM grooming." Either way it lands the section thesis: **own the void, own the answer.** Ties directly back to the Golebiewski & boyd data-void row above; keep it honest — don't assert "AI is groomed by Russia" as settled. |
| **Data Voids in News Portals** | Fischer, Jaidka & Lelkes, ["Auditing local news presence on Google News"](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41562-020-0838-5) (*Nature Human Behaviour*, 2020) — related methods on Google News personalization; and Feamster-lab / collaborators' cross-country news-portal study cited in the book. The plot: tall black bars are *all* sources, colored bars (sources with >5, 10, 15, 20 articles) are much shorter — a few sources dominate the feed. Articles-per-source ranged ~130:1 (NZ, Switzerland — low diversity) to ~35:1 (US — more diverse). Some countries even saw complete filtering of certain queries. "The Internet looks very different to different people" — empirically. |
| **When the Fix Makes It Worse: Algospeak** | **Popularization:** Taylor Lorenz, ["Internet 'algospeak' is changing our language in real time"](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2022/04/08/algospeak-tiktok-le-dollar-bean/) (Washington Post, Apr 2022). **Academic:** Klug, Steen & Yurechko, ["How Algorithm Awareness Impacts Algospeak Use on TikTok"](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3543873.3587355) (WWW '23 Companion); Steen, Yurechko & Klug, ["You Can (Not) Say What You Want"](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/20563051231194586) (*Social Media + Society*, 2023). **Creator-adaptation literature:** Cotter, ["Playing the visibility game"](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1461444818815684) (*New Media & Society*, 2019); Karizat et al., ["Algorithmic Folk Theories and Identity"](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3479857) (CSCW 2021); DeVito, ["How Transfeminine TikTok Creators Navigate the Algorithmic Trap of Visibility Via Folk Theorization"](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3555105) (CSCW 2022); Duffy & Meisner, ["Platform governance at the margins"](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/01634437221111923) (*Media, Culture & Society*, 2023). Upstream infrastructure: Sarah T. Roberts, [*Behind the Screen*](https://yalebooks.yale.edu/book/9780300235883/behind-the-screen/) (Yale, 2019). Second-order filter bubble: the engagement bubble (what gets ranked up) is compounded by a self-censorship bubble (what gets made in the first place). |
| **Takeaways** | The book's three section takeaways verbatim in spirit. Land the synthesis: personalization is friction + flooding combined, it's deniable, it's an attack surface, and the policy frontier (DSA Article 40) is about making it *measurable* at all. **Live policy anchor to watch:** the Jan 22, 2026 [TikTok US divestiture deal](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/economy/tiktok-signs-deal-to-sell-u-s-unit-to-american-investors-including-oracle-and-silver-lake) — ByteDance transferred control of TikTok's US operations to a joint venture led by Oracle, Silver Lake, and MGX (ByteDance retains 19.9%; Oracle is the "trusted security partner" and will host the retrained US algorithm). Coverage: [CNBC on the signing memo](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/12/18/tik-tok-us-sale-china.html) · [Variety](https://variety.com/2025/digital/news/tiktok-us-joint-venture-deal-close-date-oracle-silver-lake-1236612315/) · [The Hill](https://thehill.com/policy/technology/5656611-tiktok-deal-divest-us-assets/). The US case study of "who controls the algorithm" moving from geopolitical fight to corporate-governance arrangement. |
| **Where This Connects** | Locate today in the larger map: Ch. 3 covers moderation (overt), information operations (flooding), personalization (quiet friction). Personalization is the hardest to see and therefore the hardest to regulate — which is exactly why the measurement (Ch. 5) and transparency-law (Ch. 4, DSA) threads matter. End by reminding them of the squirrel. |
