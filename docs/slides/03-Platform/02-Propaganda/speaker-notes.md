| **Censorship That Doesn't Block** | **Current-event hook: 2024 was the biggest election year in world history — 76 countries, ~4 billion eligible voters — and the dominant information-control tactic across it was flooding, not blocking.** Freedom House's annual [Freedom on the Net](https://freedomhouse.org/report/freedom-net) documents that AI-generated content and coordinated inauthentic behavior touched a large share of 2024 elections. [V-Dem's Democracy Reports](https://v-dem.net/publications/democracy-reports/) track the same pattern: overt blocks are the *outlier* now; the everyday tactic is amplification of preferred narratives.<br><br>Framing: Ch. 2 and most of moderation is about subtraction — blocking, throttling, takedowns. Flooding is addition. Tie back to the "tax on access" thesis from Lecture 1: flooding raises the cost of *finding the truth* rather than the cost of *reaching a site*. Book §3.3 (Flooding). |
| **Roberts: Fear, Friction, Flooding** | **Current-event hook: one live case per leg of the trichotomy, all from the last ~18 months.**<br><br>**Fear** — Turkey's ongoing prosecution of journalists and social-media users under its 2022 "disinformation law" (Law No. 7418, amending the Turkish Press Law) accelerated through 2024–25; see [Freedom House — Turkey FoTN 2024](https://freedomhouse.org/country/turkey/freedom-net/2024) (rates Turkey "Not Free," documents 953k+ blocked domains as of Dec 2023) and [CPJ Turkey coverage](https://cpj.org/europe/turkey/) (documents prosecutions of journalists under the law). India's [IT Rules, 2021](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Information_Technology_Rules,_2021) and the 2023 "Fact Check Unit" (struck down by the Bombay HC in Sep 2024) exemplify the *chilling* half of fear.<br><br>**Friction** — Russia's slow-motion YouTube kill (Jul 2024 – 2025), covered in Lecture 3: throttling instead of blocking is the friction playbook. Sources: [Carnegie Endowment (Feb 2025)](https://carnegieendowment.org/russia-eurasia/politika/2025/02/russia-youtube-block-attempt?lang=en) · [Blocking of YouTube in Russia (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blocking_of_YouTube_in_Russia).<br><br>**Flooding** — the OpenAI [June 2025 threat report](https://openai.com/global-affairs/disrupting-malicious-uses-of-ai-june-2025/) (10 campaigns, 6 countries) + Meta's [quarterly Adversarial Threat Reports](https://transparency.meta.com/metasecurity/threat-reporting) show flooding as the modal tactic of covert operations in 2024–25.<br><br>Underlying framing: Roberts, [*Censored: Distraction and Diversion Inside China's Great Firewall*](https://press.princeton.edu/books/hardcover/9780691178868/censored) (Princeton, 2018) — the course spine; Chapter 2 introduces the fear/friction/flooding trichotomy. Companion: Benkler, Faris & Roberts, [*Network Propaganda*](https://global.oup.com/academic/product/network-propaganda-9780190923624) (Oxford, 2018). |
| **What Is Propaganda?** | **Marquee current-event anchor: X's dismantling of state-media labels (Apr 2023) and the subsequent RT/Sputnik resurgence.** X removed "state-affiliated media" labels for RT, Sputnik, Xinhua and others in April 2023 — widely covered by Reuters, NPR, and Poynter at the time (search "Twitter state-affiliated media labels April 2023"). The label removal is the exact policy lever that operationalizes the propaganda-is-old-but-cheap-now argument: same content, less friction on reaching audiences. A second live example: **Grok's July 2025 "MechaHitler" episode** — xAI's chatbot generated antisemitic content after a system-prompt change, showing propaganda-adjacent output *emerging from the platform itself*, not just carried by it — see [Wikipedia — Grok (chatbot)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grok_(chatbot)).<br><br>Underlying theory (background): Benkler, Faris & Roberts, [*Network Propaganda*](https://global.oup.com/academic/product/network-propaganda-9780190923624) (Oxford, 2018) — propaganda is *old* but the barrier to entry collapsed. Historical anchor: Jacques Ellul, [*Propaganda: The Formation of Men's Attitudes*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propaganda:_The_Formation_of_Men%27s_Attitudes) (1965). Distinguish from disinformation (false) — propaganda need not be false. |
| **When Blocking Backfires** | **Marquee current-event anchor: Russia's own backfire, 2022–2025.** After banning Meta (Instagram, Facebook) in March 2022, Russia inadvertently drove a massive spike in VPN adoption and — more importantly — VPN users gained access to *everything* blocked in Russia, not just Instagram — see [Internet censorship in Russia (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_censorship_in_Russia). The 2024–25 YouTube throttling campaign (covered in Lecture 3) is the counter-move: Russia learned that outright blocks generate exactly the backfire Roberts predicted, so they moved to throttling and now to flooding domestic replacements (VK, RuTube). Iran ran a similar arc after the [2022–23 Mahsa Amini protests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahsa_Amini_protests): aggressive blocking triggered VPN uptake that outlasted the protests — [Freedom House — Iran FoTN 2024](https://freedomhouse.org/country/iran/freedom-net/2024) documents 30–40% ISP cost hikes and a Feb 2024 unlicensed-VPN ban that came after the earlier crackdown failed.<br><br>Underlying theory: Roberts's Instagram-in-China 2014 empirical case in [*Censored*](https://press.princeton.edu/books/hardcover/9780691178868/censored), Ch. 4 — about half of active users simply left, the other half jumped the wall with VPNs and gained access to *everything* behind the Firewall. Flooding avoids this backfire entirely. |
| **Flooding Is the "Uncensored" Internet** | **Marquee current-event anchor: Meta and OpenAI keep taking down Chinese flooding networks.** Meta has publicly attributed the **"Spamouflage" / Dragonbridge** network (aka Storm-1376) — the largest known covert influence operation — to Chinese law-enforcement actors, with takedowns in every Meta [Adversarial Threat Report](https://transparency.meta.com/metasecurity/threat-reporting) since 2019 and continuing through 2024–25. OpenAI's [June 2025 threat intel report](https://openai.com/global-affairs/disrupting-malicious-uses-of-ai-june-2025/) documents "Sneer Review," a China-linked operation that used ChatGPT to generate TikTok/X/Reddit/Facebook posts *and* their own replies — manufactured consensus at model speed. Microsoft's Threat Analysis Center tracks the same actors as **Storm-1376** — see [Microsoft — East Asia influence operations report (Apr 2024)](https://blogs.microsoft.com/on-the-issues/2024/04/04/china-ai-influence-elections-mtac-cybersecurity/), which documented Chinese AI-generated content aimed at Taiwan's Jan 2024 election and the US 2024 cycle.<br><br>Underlying theory: King, Pan & Roberts, ["How the Chinese Government Fabricates Social Media Posts for Strategic Distraction, Not Engaged Argument"](https://gking.harvard.edu/files/gking/files/50c.pdf) (*APSR*, 2017). The "50-cent army" doesn't argue with dissidents; it changes the subject. Roughly 448 million fabricated posts per year — cheerleading + distraction, not rebuttal. The 2024–25 Spamouflage/Sneer Review operations are the same playbook, now automated. |
| **Why Flooding Is So Dangerous** | **Marquee current-event anchor: the EU DSA's systemic-risk regime treats flooding as a live regulatory problem.** Under Articles 34–35 of the [Digital Services Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_Services_Act), Very Large Online Platforms (VLOPs) must assess and mitigate "systemic risks" including manipulation of information — the first legal regime to name *aggregate information-environment harm* as regulable. The Commission has opened formal proceedings against X (Dec 2023, expanded 2024), TikTok (Feb 2024; also the [Dec 2024 Romania election proceeding](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/news/commission-opens-formal-proceedings-against-tiktok-election-risks-under-digital-services-act)), Meta, and AliExpress under this authority — see [European Commission — Enforcement of the DSA](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/dsa-enforcement). The UK's [Online Safety Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Online_Safety_Act_2023) illegal-content duties came into force March 2025 with a parallel framing.<br><br>Underlying theory: Book §3.3 takeaways — legal frameworks are built around removing specific harmful content; flooding has no specific harmful content to point at. Analytical frame: Wu, ["Is the First Amendment Obsolete?"](https://knightcolumbia.org/content/is-the-first-amendment-obsolete) (Knight First Amendment Institute, 2017) — the modern threat is not too little speech but too much, weaponized. The DSA is what Wu's essay looks like when a legislature actually acts. |
| **Flooding Predates the Internet** | **Current-event hook: 2024–26 is "the AI slop year" — the modern analog to tobacco PR floods, at zero marginal cost.** [404 Media's AI Slop coverage](https://www.404media.co/tag/ai-slop/) documents AI-generated garbage saturating Facebook (the "Shrimp Jesus" phenomenon), Google search results, Amazon product listings, and self-published books. NewsGuard's [AI Tracking Center](https://www.newsguardtech.com/special-reports/ai-tracking-center/) catalogs **3,749 AI-generated "content farm" news sites across 16 languages as of Jun 2026**, up from zero in early 2023. The channel is *already* noisy from commercial flooding before any political actor arrives — which is why political flooding is so cheap to add on top.<br><br>Framing: de-exoticize the phenomenon. The attention economy means online newspapers are already flooding us before any bad actor shows up. Background reading: Tim Wu, [*The Attention Merchants*](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/236941/the-attention-merchants-by-tim-wu/) (Knopf, 2016). Pro sports and tobacco press-release history is documented in the marketing-studies literature; Wu cites both. |
| **The 2012 Insight: Behavior, Not Content** | **Marquee current-event anchor: behavioral signatures are still exactly what platform threat teams use in 2025.** Meta's quarterly [Adversarial Threat Reports](https://transparency.meta.com/metasecurity/threat-reporting) and OpenAI's [June 2025 report](https://openai.com/global-affairs/disrupting-malicious-uses-of-ai-june-2025/) both define "coordinated inauthentic behavior" (CIB) explicitly by *behavior* — the accounts' posting cadence, mutual reply patterns, shared infrastructure — not by *content*. Cross-platform: [DFRLab's Digital Forensic Research Lab dispatches](https://dfrlab.org/) routinely surface CIB networks first via behavioral patterns (identical timestamps, mutual retweets, IP overlap). The 2012 detector no longer works verbatim, but the *methodological bet* has been fully vindicated.<br><br>Underlying theory: Ratkiewicz, Conover, Meiss, Gonçalves, Flammini & Menczer, ["Detecting and Tracking Political Abuse in Social Media"](https://ojs.aaai.org/index.php/ICWSM/article/view/14127) (ICWSM 2011); companion piece ["Truthy: Mapping the Spread of Astroturf in Microblog Streams"](https://arxiv.org/abs/1011.3768) (WWW 2011). Datasets: 2010 Nevada Senate race (#nvsen) and the debt-ceiling debate (#debtceiling). The *principle* is what matters; the specific classifier is dated. |
| **Posting Is Wildly Skewed** | **Marquee current-event anchor: [Reuters Institute Digital News Report 2025](https://reutersinstitute.politics.ox.ac.uk/digital-news-report/2025)** — the 40+ market study shows global trust in social-media news continuing to drop while a small hyperactive tail drives most political conversation. Pew Research's ongoing tracker of US X users has for years shown the top ~20% of adult users producing ~98% of posts (see the [Pew Research Center — social media landing page](https://www.pewresearch.org/topic/internet-technology/) for the current studies); the "post-once" majority and the tiny hyperactive tail are more extreme now than they were in 2012.<br><br>Underlying theory: same #nvsen / Truthy CDFs from Ratkiewicz et al. above. Read the CDF: ~50% of users posted exactly once while a tiny tail posted hundreds of times. That heavy tail is where coordinated amplification lives. Academic follow-on: [Bail et al., "Exposure to opposing views on social media"](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1804840115) (PNAS 2018). |
| **From #bias to Information Operations** | **Marquee current-event case: the Doppelganger operation and Germany's Feb 2025 election.** Kremlin-linked Social Design Agency operation (established 2022, sanctioned by EU Aug 2024 and Dec 16 2024). CeMAS documented 630+ Doppelganger-attributed posts on X between Dec 2024 and Jan 2025 boosting AfD ahead of the Feb 23, 2025 vote — see [Euronews coverage](https://www.euronews.com/my-europe/2025/01/22/russias-disinformation-operations-resurface-ahead-of-german-election) and [Insikt Group / Recorded Future](https://www.recordedfuture.com/research/stimmen-aus-moskau-russian-influence-operations-target-german-elections). Overview: [Doppelganger (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doppelganger_(disinformation_campaign)) · [OSW analysis](https://www.osw.waw.pl/en/publikacje/analyses/2024-09-13/doppelganger-pattern-russias-anti-western-influence-operation) · [EU DisinfoLab original 2022 unmasking](https://www.disinfo.eu/doppelganger/). **Brazil Aug 30 – Oct 8, 2024:** [X ban](https://www.lawfaremedia.org/article/musk-s-x-banned-in-brazil) — the culmination of TSE's 1,000+ takedown orders. **2016 Russia (IRA):** Senate Select Committee on Intelligence Report Vol. 2, ["Russia's Use of Social Media"](https://www.intelligence.senate.gov/sites/default/files/documents/Report_Volume2.pdf) (2019) is the primary source.<br><br>The tactics are stable; the actors and venues change — that is the point of the arc. |
| **Bots: Automated and Human** | **Marquee current-event anchor: Meta and OpenAI 2024–25 threat reports both say the most effective operations are human-directed.** Meta's [Adversarial Threat Reports](https://transparency.meta.com/metasecurity/threat-reporting) through 2024–25 consistently document that fully-automated networks are relatively easy to detect and take down; the persistent, effective operations mix a small human team with cheap automation. OpenAI's [June 2025 report](https://openai.com/global-affairs/disrupting-malicious-uses-of-ai-june-2025/) notes the same pattern — human operators using ChatGPT as a productivity tool, not as a hands-off bot swarm. Stress that "bot" is overloaded and increasingly misleading. Keep students skeptical of breathless "bots swung the election" claims; the honest answer is effects are contested.<br><br>Underlying theory: Ferrara, Varol, Davis, Menczer & Flammini, ["The Rise of Social Bots"](https://cacm.acm.org/magazines/2016/7/204021-the-rise-of-social-bots/fulltext) (*CACM*, 2016). Counterpoint (exposure ≠ persuasion): Bail et al., ["Assessing the Russian Internet Research Agency's impact on the political attitudes and behaviors of American Twitter users"](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1906420116) (PNAS 2020) — found no measurable attitude change from IRA exposure. |
| **What Counts as Disinformation?** | **Marquee current-event anchor: Murthy v. Missouri, decided June 26, 2024.** The US Supreme Court ruled 6–3 that plaintiffs lacked standing to enjoin federal officials from communicating with platforms about "misinformation" — see [Wikipedia — Murthy v. Missouri](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Murthy_v._Missouri). Background analysis at the [Knight First Amendment Institute](https://knightcolumbia.org/). The case *is* the "who gets to define disinformation" fight made legal. Alongside it: the [Stanford Internet Observatory shutdown (Jun 2024)](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2024/06/14/stanford-internet-observatory-disinformation-research-lawsuits-politics/) — SIO and the Election Integrity Partnership were dismantled after congressional subpoenas from Rep. Jim Jordan's committee and lawsuits from America First Legal argued researcher/platform coordination violated First Amendment rights. See [Wikipedia — Stanford Internet Observatory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stanford_Internet_Observatory) · [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2024/06/14/g-s1-4570/a-major-disinformation-research-teams-future-is-uncertain-after-political-attacks) · [Platformer](https://www.platformer.news/stanford-internet-observatory-shutdown-stamos-diresta-sio/).<br><br>Underlying theory: Fallis, ["What Is Disinformation?"](https://muse.jhu.edu/article/579342) (*Library Trends*, 2015) — the three-condition definition. Cross-reference: Wardle & Derakhshan, ["Information Disorder"](https://rm.coe.int/information-disorder-toward-an-interdisciplinary-framework-for-researc/168076277c) (Council of Europe, 2017) — the widely used mis/dis/mal-information taxonomy. The hard cases live at the boundaries: disambiguating disinformation from satire is brutal for automated systems (Poe's Law). The most politically charged mistake is conflating partisan bias with disinformation. |
| **Who Spreads It, and Why** | **Marquee current-event anchor: the January 2024 New Hampshire Biden deepfake robocall.** Steve Kramer, a political consultant working for a Dean Phillips super-PAC ally, hired a magician to clone Biden's voice with ElevenLabs and robocalled New Hampshire voters urging them to skip the Jan 23, 2024 primary. FCC fined Lingo Telecom $1M in Aug 2024 for transmitting the calls; Kramer was fined $6M by the FCC and criminally indicted in New Hampshire (search "FCC Lingo Telecom Biden robocall" for FCC press release + coverage). This is the paradigmatic "profit-adjacent + political" hybrid motive. Companion: the [October 2023 Slovakia election deepfake audio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Slovak_parliamentary_election) of Progressive Slovakia leader Michal Šimečka discussing rigging the vote — released 48 hours before the election, when Slovak law prohibits political advertising, so nothing could be officially rebutted. The 2016 [Macedonian teens / Veles clickbait](https://www.wired.com/2017/02/veles-macedonia-fake-news/) remains the classic pure-profit case for comparison.<br><br>Motives span profit / politics / status. Structural enablers matter most for the course: the collapse of local news and platform consolidation create the data voids and attention dynamics that disinformation exploits. Reference: Abernathy et al., ["The State of Local News"](https://localnewsinitiative.northwestern.edu/projects/state-of-local-news/2024/report/) (Northwestern Medill, 2024). |
| **Effects of Propaganda** | **Marquee current-event anchor: 2024 Moldova, Georgia, Romania — three elections where Russian interference clearly aimed at demobilization, not persuasion.** Moldova's Oct 2024 EU-accession referendum passed by a razor-thin margin after documented Russian vote-buying and disinformation (well covered by Politico, Reuters, Foreign Policy — search "Moldova EU referendum October 2024 Russia interference"). Georgia's Oct 2024 parliamentary election fit the same pattern. The [Dec 2024 Romania election annulment](https://www.atlanticcouncil.org/blogs/new-atlanticist/romania-annulled-its-presidential-election-results-amid-alleged-russian-interference-what-happens-next/) is the clearest instance yet — see [2024 Romanian presidential election (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_Romanian_presidential_election). In each case the observable goal is a disoriented, distrustful electorate that turns out unevenly or not at all — Applebaum's demobilization thesis in the wild.<br><br>Underlying theory: Applebaum, [*Autocracy, Inc.*](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/671541/autocracy-inc-by-anne-applebaum/) (2024) — makes the demobilization argument explicitly for post-2020 autocracies. Also Benkler et al. 2018. Connect back to Roberts: distraction *is* flooding. |
| **Other Manipulation Tactics** | **Marquee current-event anchor: Google's AI Overviews launched May 2024 and immediately produced viral errors — pizza glue, rocks-are-good-to-eat.** See [Wikipedia — AI Overviews](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AI_Overviews), which documents the "eat rocks" and glue-in-pizza incidents and the multiple lawsuits filed against Google. This is *layout change as manipulation vector* — Google didn't change what pages exist, it changed what's presented in the answer position. Cross-reference: [Perplexity AI (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perplexity_AI) covers the 2024–25 lawsuits from NYT, BBC, and newspaper publishers over content scraping and citation gaming — adversaries also exploiting *presentation*, not content. The rise of AI answer engines makes the "change the ordering" lever more powerful than it was during the classic search era.<br><br>Underlying framing: Book §3.3 names three levers a manipulator pulls — (i) change the available choices (top-N results), (ii) change presentation/layout (ordering, imagery, font), or (iii) change semantic content (fabrication, sock-puppet endorsements). Polluting a user's profile changes how *future* results rank — a bridge to Lecture 6 (personalization). Canonical academic reference: Xing et al., ["Take This Personally: Pollution Attacks on Personalized Services"](https://www.usenix.org/conference/usenixsecurity13/technical-sessions/presentation/xing) (USENIX Security 2013). |
| **Disinformation Is the New Spam** | Methodological bet from the Feamster lab: detect disinformation from *how* it's sent, not *what* it says — the way we learned to detect spam from IP reputation. **Modern anchor:** the same "behavior + infrastructure" framing is now how OpenAI, Meta, and Microsoft describe their CIB detection in their 2024–25 threat reports (see [Meta transparency reports](https://transparency.meta.com/metasecurity/threat-reporting) and [OpenAI June 2025 report](https://openai.com/global-affairs/disrupting-malicious-uses-of-ai-june-2025/)). Underlying work: Hao et al., ["Detecting Spammers with SNARE"](https://www.usenix.org/legacy/event/sec09/tech/full_papers/hao.pdf) (USENIX Security 2009). Why this matters: early detection. Once disinformation spreads, in some sense it's already too late — content-level debunking arrives after the damage. |
| **Infrastructure Signals of Disinformation** | Hounsel, Holland, Kaiser, Borgolte, Feamster & Mayer, ["Identifying Disinformation Websites Using Infrastructure Features"](https://www.usenix.org/conference/foci20/presentation/hounsel) (FOCI 2020) — content-agnostic features. Nice surprise: legitimate news orgs have *long* SAN lists (lots of shared certs), which is itself a signal. The WordPress gap is the most intuitive one. **Modern anchor:** the same infrastructure-clustering approach is what NewsGuard used in its 2024–25 [Pravda / Portal Kombat mapping](https://www.newsguardrealitycheck.com/p/a-well-funded-moscow-based-global) (identifying ~150 sites via infra + template overlap). |
| **It Works — Mostly** | ROC on the left, precision-recall on the right (Hounsel et al. FOCI 2020). "All" features combined hit AUC ~0.95 — strong for a fully automated, content-free classifier. **Modern caveat with real data:** adversaries adapt — infrastructure can be changed once it's a known tell. Same arms-race dynamic as spam. Recent example: the Pravda network operators moved to lower-fingerprint hosting after the [March 2025 NewsGuard exposé](https://www.newsguardrealitycheck.com/p/a-well-funded-moscow-based-global). Honest caveat: this is an *early-warning* signal, not ground truth. |
| **The Hard Part Is Definition** | **Marquee current-event anchor: Murthy v. Missouri (SCOTUS, Jun 26 2024) + the SIO shutdown (Jun 2024).** Two events in the same week made the point that the definitional fight is now itself a political-legal fight, not a technical one. [Murthy v. Missouri](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Murthy_v._Missouri): SCOTUS ruled the plaintiffs lacked standing, leaving federal-platform communication legally intact — but the *chilling* effect on those conversations is well documented. [SIO shutdown](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2024/06/14/stanford-internet-observatory-disinformation-research-lawsuits-politics/): Stanford's Internet Observatory and the Election Integrity Partnership were dismantled after congressional subpoenas from Rep. Jim Jordan's committee and lawsuits from America First Legal argued researcher/platform coordination violated First Amendment rights. See [Wikipedia — Stanford Internet Observatory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stanford_Internet_Observatory) · [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2024/06/14/g-s1-4570/a-major-disinformation-research-teams-future-is-uncertain-after-political-attacks) · [Platformer](https://www.platformer.news/stanford-internet-observatory-shutdown-stamos-diresta-sio/) · [NYU Stern Center take](https://bhr.stern.nyu.edu/quick-take/the-destruction-of-a-research-group-at-stanford-marks-a-dangerous-partisan-victory/).<br><br>End the detection arc on humility: technical problem is tractable; definitional and normative problems are not. Every detector encodes a contested definition, and aggressive early detection trades off against over-removal of legitimate speech. This is a Ch. 4 (legal) preview. |
| **AI-Generated Flooding** | **OpenAI, ["Disrupting malicious uses of AI: June 2025"](https://openai.com/global-affairs/disrupting-malicious-uses-of-ai-june-2025/)** — 10 malicious AI campaigns disrupted in the prior 3 months across 6 countries (China, Russia, North Korea, Iran, Cambodia, Philippines). Four cases likely China-origin. "Sneer Review" used ChatGPT to generate TikTok/X/Reddit/Facebook posts *and* their own replies, faking organic engagement. OpenAI even recovered an internal performance-review document the operators had ChatGPT draft. Report PDF: [Disrupting malicious uses of AI, June 2025](https://cdn.openai.com/threat-intelligence-reports/5f73af09-a3a3-4a55-992e-069237681620/disrupting-malicious-uses-of-ai-june-2025.pdf). Tie back to the 2012 signatures: posting a comment *and* its own replies is manufactured consensus — the same behavior, now automated. Also relevant: [Meta transparency threat reporting](https://transparency.meta.com/metasecurity/threat-reporting) documents Doppelganger tactical adaptations, increased GenAI use across CIB networks, and CIB networks' persistent inability to build authentic audiences. **Romania Dec 2024:** Constitutional Court annulled the presidential election after intelligence documents showed Russia-linked coordinated TikTok amplification of Călin Georgescu — see [Atlantic Council](https://www.atlanticcouncil.org/blogs/new-atlanticist/romania-annulled-its-presidential-election-results-amid-alleged-russian-interference-what-happens-next/) · [Global Witness investigation](https://globalwitness.org/en/campaigns/digital-threats/what-happened-on-tiktok-around-the-annulled-romanian-presidential-election-an-investigation-and-poll/) · [2024 Romanian presidential election (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_Romanian_presidential_election). First time an EU election was annulled over algorithmic amplification. |
| **Grooming the Chatbots** | **NewsGuard, March 2025:** [Pravda network / Portal Kombat report](https://www.newsguardrealitycheck.com/p/a-well-funded-moscow-based-global) — ~150 sites, 3.6M+ articles in 2024, chatbots echoed Pravda claims in ~33% of relevant prompts (10 chatbots tested including OpenAI, Microsoft, Google, Anthropic, Meta, xAI, Perplexity, Mistral, You.com). Term "LLM grooming" coined by the [American Sunlight Project (Feb 2025)](https://www.americansunlight.org/updates/new-report-the-pravda-network-and-its-role-in-training-ai). Coverage: [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2025/03/06/exclusive-russian-disinfo-floods-ai-chatbots-study-finds) · [Bulletin of the Atomic Scientists](https://thebulletin.org/2025/03/russian-networks-flood-the-internet-with-propaganda-aiming-to-corrupt-ai-chatbots/). **Contested:** [Kertysova et al., "LLM grooming or data voids?" (Harvard *Misinformation Review*, 2025)](https://misinforeview.hks.harvard.edu/article/llms-grooming-or-data-voids-llm-powered-chatbot-references-to-kremlin-disinformation-reflect-information-gaps-not-manipulation/) argues the effect reflects **data voids**, not successful grooming — chatbots reproduce Pravda content mainly when prompts probe obscure claims with no mainstream sources. See also [Al Jazeera critique](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2025/7/8/is-russia-really-grooming-western). Teaching move: report the verified facts (network exists, NewsGuard number, rebuttal exists); the causal claim is open. The flooding target is moving up the stack — pollute the *training and retrieval data* so the AI launders your narrative. |
| **Takeaways** | Book §3.3 close. Flooding may be the most dangerous form because it's legal, deniable, and hard to detect.
