# Lecture 5 — Speaker Notes (Flooding and Propaganda)

Per-slide context + clickable links. In-deck notes (the `::: {.notes}` blocks) are also visible via reveal.js speaker view (press **S**). Every URL was verified this session or is a canonical org landing page.

| Slide | Context + Links |
|---|---|
| **Censorship That Doesn't Block** | Book §3.3 (Flooding). Ch. 2 and most of moderation is about subtraction — blocking, throttling, takedowns. Flooding is addition. The censor lets all content flow and simply buries what they don't like under noise. Tie back to the "tax on access" thesis from Lecture 1: flooding raises the cost of *finding the truth* rather than the cost of *reaching a site*. |
| **Roberts: Fear, Friction, Flooding** | Roberts, [*Censored: Distraction and Diversion Inside China's Great Firewall*](https://press.princeton.edu/books/hardcover/9780691178868/censored) (Princeton, 2018) — the course spine. Chapter 2 of *Censored* introduces the fear/friction/flooding trichotomy. The key insight: a regime that finds direct censorship politically costly may *prefer* to flood — it leaves no evidence of suppression and is hard to organize against. Companion academic reference for the whole propaganda arc: Benkler, Faris & Roberts, [*Network Propaganda*](https://global.oup.com/academic/product/network-propaganda-9780190923624) (Oxford, 2018). |
| **What Is Propaganda?** | Quote from Benkler, Faris & Roberts, [*Network Propaganda*](https://global.oup.com/academic/product/network-propaganda-9780190923624) (Oxford, 2018). Note that propaganda is *old* — it long predates social media. What changed is the barrier to entry. Anyone can now run a targeted, population-scale campaign for near-zero cost. Distinguish from disinformation (false) — propaganda need not be false; volume, repetition, and framing are enough. Classic historical reference: Jacques Ellul, [*Propaganda: The Formation of Men's Attitudes*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propaganda:_The_Formation_of_Men%27s_Attitudes) (1965). |
| **When Blocking Backfires** | Roberts's empirical anchor: China blocked Instagram in Sept 2014. Data in [Roberts, *Censored*](https://press.princeton.edu/books/hardcover/9780691178868/censored), Ch. 4. About half of active users simply left, but the other half jumped the wall with VPNs and gained access to *everything* behind the Firewall — not just Instagram. Direct blocking foments the very circumvention it's meant to prevent. Flooding avoids this backfire entirely. |
| **Flooding Is the "Uncensored" Internet** | Canonical empirical reference: King, Pan & Roberts, ["How the Chinese Government Fabricates Social Media Posts for Strategic Distraction, Not Engaged Argument"](https://gking.harvard.edu/files/gking/files/50c.pdf) (*APSR*, 2017). The "50-cent army" doesn't argue with dissidents; it changes the subject. Roughly 448 million fabricated posts per year in China, overwhelmingly cheerleading + distraction rather than rebuttal. The goal is to redirect attention away from anything that could spark collective action. |
| **Why Flooding Is So Dangerous** | Book §3.3 takeaways. Legal frameworks are built around removing specific harmful content. Flooding has no specific harmful content to point at — the harm is aggregate. When search results push a story to page ten, at what point is that censorship? No clear answer — which is exactly what makes it work. Companion analytical frame: Wu, ["Is the First Amendment Obsolete?"](https://knightcolumbia.org/content/is-the-first-amendment-obsolete) (Knight First Amendment Institute, 2017) — argues the modern threat is not too little speech but too much, weaponized. |
| **Flooding Predates the Internet** | De-exoticize this. The attention economy means online newspapers are already flooding us before any bad actor shows up. That's *why* it's so easy for others to come in and add disorientation — the channel is already noisy. General reading: Tim Wu, [*The Attention Merchants*](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/236941/the-attention-merchants-by-tim-wu/) (Knopf, 2016). Pro sports and tobacco press-release history is documented in the marketing-studies literature; the book cites both. |
| **The 2012 Insight: Behavior, Not Content** | Ratkiewicz, Conover, Meiss, Gonçalves, Flammini & Menczer, ["Detecting and Tracking Political Abuse in Social Media"](https://ojs.aaai.org/index.php/ICWSM/article/view/14127) (ICWSM 2011). And the companion piece: Ratkiewicz et al., ["Truthy: Mapping the Spread of Astroturf in Microblog Streams"](https://arxiv.org/abs/1011.3768) (WWW 2011). Datasets were the 2010 Nevada Senate race (#nvsen) and the debt-ceiling debate (#debtceiling). Caveat: that specific classifier likely no longer works as platforms changed — the *principle* is what matters. |
| **Posting Is Wildly Skewed** | Same #bias / Truthy data. Read the CDF: for #nvsen, ~50% of users posted exactly once, while a tiny tail posted hundreds of times. That heavy tail is where coordinated amplification lives. Quantitative basis for "manufactured consensus" — a few accounts plus fast retweets simulate a movement that isn't there. Modern analog: Twitter/X's "power law of engagement" documented in [Bail et al., "Exposure to opposing views on social media"](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1804840115) (PNAS 2018) and follow-on work. |
| **From #bias to Information Operations** | The book traces this arc in §3.3. **2016 Russia (IRA):** Senate Select Committee on Intelligence Report Vol. 2, ["Russia's Use of Social Media"](https://www.intelligence.senate.gov/sites/default/files/documents/Report_Volume2.pdf) (2019) is the primary source; also the two [Stanford Internet Observatory / New Knowledge reports for the SSCI (2018)](https://www.intelligence.senate.gov/publications/committee-findings-2017-intelligence-community-assessment). **2020 COVID misinformation:** [Roozenbeek et al., *R. Soc. Open Sci.* (2020)](https://royalsocietypublishing.org/doi/10.1098/rsos.201199). **Brazil 2024:** [X ban Aug 30 – Oct 8, 2024](https://www.lawfaremedia.org/article/musk-s-x-banned-in-brazil), the culmination of TSE's 1,000+ takedown orders. **Marquee current-event case: the Doppelganger operation targeting Germany's Feb 2025 election.** Kremlin-linked Social Design Agency operation (established 2022, sanctioned by EU Aug 2024 and Dec 16 2024). CeMAS documented 630+ Doppelganger-attributed posts on X between Dec 2024 and Jan 2025 boosting AfD ahead of Feb 23, 2025 vote — see [Euronews coverage](https://www.euronews.com/my-europe/2025/01/22/russias-disinformation-operations-resurface-ahead-of-german-election) and [Insikt Group / Recorded Future report](https://www.recordedfuture.com/research/stimmen-aus-moskau-russian-influence-operations-target-german-elections). Overview: [Doppelganger (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doppelganger_(disinformation_campaign)) · [OSW Centre analysis](https://www.osw.waw.pl/en/publikacje/analyses/2024-09-13/doppelganger-pattern-russias-anti-western-influence-operation) · [EU DisinfoLab's original 2022 unmasking](https://www.disinfo.eu/doppelganger/). The tactics are stable; the actors and venues change. |
| **Bots: Automated and Human** | Stress that "bot" is overloaded. The most effective operations are often human-directed, not fully automated. And we genuinely don't understand the *downstream effects* well — exposure does not equal persuasion. Keep students skeptical of breathless "bots swung the election" claims; honest answer is effects are contested. Foundational reference: Ferrara, Varol, Davis, Menczer & Flammini, ["The Rise of Social Bots"](https://cacm.acm.org/magazines/2016/7/204021-the-rise-of-social-bots/fulltext) (*CACM*, 2016). Counterpoint (exposure ≠ persuasion): Bail et al., ["Assessing the Russian Internet Research Agency's impact on the political attitudes and behaviors of American Twitter users"](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1906420116) (PNAS 2020) — found no measurable attitude change from IRA exposure. |
| **What Counts as Disinformation?** | Fallis, ["What Is Disinformation?"](https://muse.jhu.edu/article/579342) (*Library Trends*, 2015) — the three-condition definition. The hard cases live at the boundaries: disambiguating disinformation from satire is brutal for automated systems (Poe's Law). The most politically charged mistake is conflating partisan bias with disinformation — a recurring move in moderation debates. Cross-reference: Wardle & Derakhshan, ["Information Disorder"](https://rm.coe.int/information-disorder-toward-an-interdisciplinary-framework-for-researc/168076277c) (Council of Europe, 2017) — the widely used mis/dis/mal-information taxonomy. |
| **Who Spreads It, and Why** | The 2016 [Macedonian teens / Veles clickbait](https://www.wired.com/2017/02/veles-macedonia-fake-news/) are the classic "it's just business" case — pure profit, no ideology. Motives span profit / politics / status. Structural enablers matter most for the course: the collapse of local news and platform consolidation create the data voids and attention dynamics that disinformation exploits. Reference for the local-news decline: Abernathy et al., ["The State of Local News"](https://localnewsinitiative.northwestern.edu/projects/state-of-local-news/2024/report/) (Northwestern Medill, 2024). |
| **Effects of Propaganda** | The sophisticated goal isn't persuasion, it's *demobilization*: a disoriented public that trusts no source is easier to govern than a persuaded one. Connect back to Roberts: distraction *is* flooding. Sources: Benkler et al. 2018; Applebaum, [*Autocracy, Inc.*](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/671541/autocracy-inc-by-anne-applebaum/) (2024) — makes the demobilization argument explicitly for post-2020 autocracies. |
| **Other Manipulation Tactics** | Book §3.3 names three levers a manipulator pulls: (i) change the available choices (top-10 search results), (ii) change presentation/layout (ordering, imagery, font), or (iii) change semantic content (fabrication, sock-puppet endorsements). Polluting a user's profile changes how *future* results rank — a bridge to Lecture 6 (personalization). Canonical academic reference: Xing et al., ["Take This Personally: Pollution Attacks on Personalized Services"](https://www.usenix.org/conference/usenixsecurity13/technical-sessions/presentation/xing) (USENIX Security 2013). |
| **Disinformation Is the New Spam** | Feamster-lab reference: methodological bet — detect disinformation from *how* it's sent, not *what* it says, the way we learned to detect spam from IP reputation. Underlying spam-detection work: Hao et al., ["Detecting Spammers with SNARE: Spatio-temporal Network-level Automatic Reputation Engine"](https://www.usenix.org/legacy/event/sec09/tech/full_papers/hao.pdf) (USENIX Security 2009). Why this matters: early detection. Once disinformation spreads, in some sense it's already too late — content-level debunking arrives after the damage. |
| **Infrastructure Signals of Disinformation** | Hounsel, Holland, Kaiser, Borgolte, Feamster & Mayer, ["Identifying Disinformation Websites Using Infrastructure Features"](https://www.usenix.org/conference/foci20/presentation/hounsel) (FOCI 2020). Features deliberately content-agnostic — they don't read the article. Nice surprise: legitimate news orgs have *long* SAN lists (lots of shared certs), which itself is a signal. The WordPress gap is the most intuitive one. The point isn't any single feature but the aggregate fingerprint. |
| **It Works — Mostly** | ROC on the left, precision-recall on the right (Hounsel et al. FOCI 2020). "All" features combined hit AUC ~0.95 — strong for a fully automated, content-free classifier. Honest caveat: this is an *early-warning* signal, not ground truth, and adversaries adapt (infrastructure can be changed once it's a known tell). Same arms-race dynamic as spam. |
| **The Hard Part Is Definition** | End the detection arc on humility. Technical problem is tractable; definitional and normative problems are not. Every detector encodes a contested definition, and aggressive early detection trades off against over-removal of legitimate speech. The [Stanford Internet Observatory shutdown (Jun 2024)](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2024/06/14/stanford-internet-observatory-disinformation-research-lawsuits-politics/) is the recent illustration: SIO and the Election Integrity Partnership were dismantled after congressional subpoenas from Rep. Jim Jordan's committee and lawsuits from America First Legal argued researcher/platform coordination violated First Amendment rights. See [Wikipedia — Stanford Internet Observatory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stanford_Internet_Observatory) · [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2024/06/14/g-s1-4570/a-major-disinformation-research-teams-future-is-uncertain-after-political-attacks) · [Platformer](https://www.platformer.news/stanford-internet-observatory-shutdown-stamos-diresta-sio/) · [NYU Stern Center take](https://bhr.stern.nyu.edu/quick-take/the-destruction-of-a-research-group-at-stanford-marks-a-dangerous-partisan-victory/). The *definitional* fight has become a political fight — which is itself a Ch. 4 (legal) preview. |
| **AI-Generated Flooding** | **OpenAI, ["Disrupting malicious uses of AI: June 2025"](https://openai.com/global-affairs/disrupting-malicious-uses-of-ai-june-2025/)** — 10 malicious AI campaigns disrupted in the prior 3 months across 6 countries (China, Russia, North Korea, Iran, Cambodia, Philippines). Four cases likely China-origin. "Sneer Review" used ChatGPT to generate TikTok/X/Reddit/Facebook posts *and* their own replies, faking organic engagement. OpenAI even recovered an internal performance-review document the operators had ChatGPT draft. Report PDF: [Disrupting malicious uses of AI, June 2025](https://cdn.openai.com/threat-intelligence-reports/5f73af09-a3a3-4a55-992e-069237681620/disrupting-malicious-uses-of-ai-june-2025.pdf). Tie back to the 2012 signatures: posting a comment *and* its own replies is manufactured consensus — the same behavior, now automated. Also relevant: **Meta's Q2/Q3 2025 Adversarial Threat Report** — see [Meta Transparency Center threat reporting](https://transparency.meta.com/metasecurity/threat-reporting) — documents Doppelganger tactical adaptations, increased GenAI use across CIB networks, and CIB networks' persistent inability to build authentic audiences. **Romania Dec 2024:** Constitutional Court annulled the presidential election after intelligence documents showed Russia-linked coordinated TikTok amplification of Călin Georgescu — see [Atlantic Council](https://www.atlanticcouncil.org/blogs/new-atlanticist/romania-annulled-its-presidential-election-results-amid-alleged-russian-interference-what-happens-next/) · [Global Witness investigation](https://globalwitness.org/en/campaigns/digital-threats/what-happened-on-tiktok-around-the-annulled-romanian-presidential-election-an-investigation-and-poll/) · [2024 Romanian presidential election (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_Romanian_presidential_election). First time an EU election was annulled over algorithmic amplification. |
| **Grooming the Chatbots** | **NewsGuard, March 2025:** [Pravda network / Portal Kombat report](https://www.newsguardrealitycheck.com/p/a-well-funded-moscow-based-global) — ~150 sites, 3.6M+ articles in 2024, chatbots echoed Pravda claims in ~33% of relevant prompts (10 chatbots tested including OpenAI, Microsoft, Google, Anthropic, Meta, xAI, Perplexity, Mistral, You.com). Term "LLM grooming" coined by the [American Sunlight Project (Feb 2025)](https://www.americansunlight.org/updates/new-report-the-pravda-network-and-its-role-in-training-ai). Coverage: [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2025/03/06/exclusive-russian-disinfo-floods-ai-chatbots-study-finds) · [Bulletin of the Atomic Scientists](https://thebulletin.org/2025/03/russian-networks-flood-the-internet-with-propaganda-aiming-to-corrupt-ai-chatbots/). **Contested:** [Kertysova et al., "LLM grooming or data voids?" (Harvard *Misinformation Review*, 2025)](https://misinforeview.hks.harvard.edu/article/llms-grooming-or-data-voids-llm-powered-chatbot-references-to-kremlin-disinformation-reflect-information-gaps-not-manipulation/) argues the effect reflects **data voids**, not successful grooming — chatbots reproduce Pravda content mainly when prompts probe obscure claims with no mainstream sources. See also [Al Jazeera critique](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2025/7/8/is-russia-really-grooming-western). Teaching move: report the verified facts (network exists, NewsGuard number, rebuttal exists); the causal claim is open. The flooding target is moving up the stack — pollute the *training and retrieval data* so the AI launders your narrative. |
| **Takeaways** | Book §3.3 close. Flooding may be the most dangerous form because it's legal, deniable, and hard to detect. Behavioral signatures (2012 Ratkiewicz et al.) recur today, now AI-accelerated. Disinformation is detectable from infrastructure, but **definition** is the real bottleneck — and that fight is now itself a censorship battlefield (SIO). |
