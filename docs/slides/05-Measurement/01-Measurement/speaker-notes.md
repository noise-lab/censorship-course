# Lecture 10 — Speaker Notes (Measuring Information Controls)

Per-slide context + clickable links. In-deck notes (the `::: {.notes}` blocks) are also visible via reveal.js speaker view (press **S**). Every URL was verified this session or is a canonical org landing page.

| Slide | Context + Links |
|---|---|
| **How Do We Know?** | The book's Ch. 5 opener: information control is often designed to look like ordinary failure, and *measurement* is what turns anecdotes into evidence. Book context: §5.1. |
| **Why Measure Information Control?** | Frame measurement as serving both engineers (circumvention) and social scientists (theories of regime behavior). Diffusion frame — Roberts, [*Censored*](https://press.princeton.edu/books/hardcover/9780691178868/censored) (Princeton, 2018). Book §5.1. |
| **A Tax We Have to Detect** | Roberts's friction/flooding frame — the ambiguity is *designed*. Anchor the lecture in the year's headline shutdown data: [Access Now KeepItOn 2025 annual report](https://www.accessnow.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/KeepItOn-Internet-Shutdowns-2025-Annual-Report.pdf) — **313 shutdowns across 52 countries** in 2025 (up from 296 in 2024); Myanmar leads at 95, India at 65 (highest among democracies). |
| **OONI: The Workhorse** | The **Open Observatory of Network Interference** — the leading open-data measurement platform. Landing pages: [ooni.org](https://ooni.org/) and [OONI Explorer](https://explorer.ooni.org/). Test suite: [Web Connectivity](https://ooni.org/nettest/web-connectivity/), [WhatsApp](https://ooni.org/nettest/whatsapp/), [Signal](https://ooni.org/nettest/signal/), [Tor](https://ooni.org/nettest/tor/), [Psiphon](https://ooni.org/nettest/psiphon/). Global reach: **200+ countries since 2012**. |
| **Google Transparency Report: A Bellwether** | Coarse but useful triangulation signal. Landing: [Google Transparency Report — Traffic](https://transparencyreport.google.com/traffic/overview). Teaching point: black-box, deniable-from-the-outside — value comes from *corroboration*, not standalone attribution. |
| **Case Study: Uganda 2021** | **Marquee current-event anchor: history really did rhyme.** After Uganda's **13 January 2026** general election, OONI documented a full **five-day internet shutdown** followed by targeted blocks of **WhatsApp, Facebook, Instagram, and X** — nearly identical to the 2021 playbook, same calendar dates (Jan 13–18). Deep dive: [OONI — How Uganda blocked social media, again](https://ooni.org/post/uganda-social-media-blocked). Cross-reference: [Berkman Klein Center summary (Jan 2026)](https://cyber.harvard.edu/story/2026-01/uganda-blocked-social-media-following-2026-general-election) · [RSF report](https://rsf.org/en/uganda-blocks-social-media-and-messaging-apps-isolating-election). Proton VPN sign-ups surged **~3,000%** over baseline during the block — the "censorship increases access" paradox in real time. |
| **A Recipe: Investigating a Suspected Block** | Book §5.1 "A Walkthrough" — the spine of the whole chapter. Emphasize Step 4 (distinguish blocking from breakage) and Step 5 (bound claims to observed networks and window). |
| **Measuring DNS Manipulation: The Hard Part** | The central difficulty: legitimate CDN and load-balancing behavior means responses *routinely* disagree — that's not evidence of censorship. Book §5.2. |
| **No Single Right Answer** | Figure from the foundational paper: Pearce, Jones et al., ["Global Measurement of DNS Manipulation" (USENIX Security 2017)](https://www.usenix.org/conference/usenixsecurity17/technical-sessions/presentation/pearce). This paper introduced **Iris**, the side-channel DNS measurement system, and quantified how much legitimate variation there is in google.com resolutions worldwide. |
| **Consistency + Verifiable Checks** | Two flavors of check — weak individually, strong together. TLS cert = independently verifiable because forging a browser-trusted cert is astronomically expensive; contrast with the reverse-DNS example from the book (google.com IPs resolving to `1e100.net`). |
| **DNS Manipulation: Prevalence** | Iris results (Pearce et al., 2017 — link above). Note the *within-country* variation — not a national monolith. Categories targeted (gambling, pornography, P2P, social media, video) are consistent with book §5.2. |
| **Measuring TCP/IP: The Vantage-Point Problem** | Side channels are the workaround — and the source of the ethics debate later. Frame the trade-off: you rarely have a probe inside the censoring country. |
| **The IP ID Side Channel** | Figure from Ensafi et al., ["Analyzing the Great Firewall of China Over Space and Time" (PoPETs 2015)](https://www.petsymposium.org/popets/2015/popets-2015-0005.php) — the "spooky scan" paper. Two conditions on the reflector: IP ID must be (a) sequential and (b) global (not per-connection). |
| **Reading the Counter: Forward vs. Reverse** | Statistical hypothesis testing over many trials; validated against Citizen Lab blocklists and known-blocked Tor bridges. Bridges to the ethics section: reflector selection is restricted to *infrastructure* machines (not individuals) to minimize risk. Book §5.2. |
| **Measuring Web Filtering: Encore** | The paper: Burnett & Feamster, ["Encore: Lightweight Measurement of Web Censorship with Cross-Origin Requests" (SIGCOMM 2015)](https://conferences.sigcomm.org/sigcomm/2015/pdf/papers/p653.pdf). **This is the most ethically contested paper in the chapter.** SIGCOMM 2015 accepted it with an unprecedented *signing statement* from the PC flagging ethical concerns — a landmark moment for computer-networking research ethics. Follow-up analysis: Narayanan & Zevenbergen, ["No Encore for Encore?" (Technology Science, 2015)](https://techscience.org/a/2015121501/). Also treated as a case study in the [*Bit By Bit* textbook](https://www.bitbybitbook.com/en/1st-ed/ethics/three-examples/encore/). |
| **Other Measurement Platforms** | **Censored Planet** (University of Michigan, Roya Ensafi's lab): [censoredplanet.org](https://censoredplanet.org/) — continuous, remote, side-channel measurement at global scale; foundational paper Sundara Raman et al., ["Censored Planet: An Internet-wide, Longitudinal Censorship Observatory" (CCS 2020)](https://ensa.fi/papers/CCS20censoredplanet.pdf). **ICLab** (UMass Amherst / Stony Brook): [iclab.org](https://iclab.org/) — VPS-based probes. The three cover different points on the representativeness / coverage / risk trade-off. |
| **Transparency Reports: Promise and Problems** | Landmarks: [Google Transparency Report](https://transparencyreport.google.com/) (right-to-be-forgotten reporting; ~50% grant rate); [Meta transparency center](https://transparency.meta.com/); [Reddit transparency](https://redditinc.com/policies/transparency-report). The format-is-the-message problem: X's last full transparency report was **2017** — a lesson in how fragile voluntary disclosure is. |
| **Legal & Economic Controls Need Other Tools** | **Lumen Database** (Berkman Klein Center, Harvard): [lumendatabase.org](https://lumendatabase.org/) — millions of takedown notices, machine-readable, primary source for DMCA-abuse research. **FOSTA/SESTA (2018)** natural experiment: Craigslist personals shut down within days of passage; academic follow-ups documented displacement to overseas platforms and measurable safety harms — see [EFF's FOSTA archive](https://www.eff.org/issues/fosta) for the ongoing litigation and secondary-effects research. Platform-transparency-loss anchor: **Meta shut down CrowdTangle on August 14, 2024**, just months before the largest election year in history — see [Nieman Lab's coverage](https://www.niemanlab.org/2024/03/a-window-into-facebook-closes-as-meta-sets-a-date-to-shut-down-crowdtangle/) and [Columbia Journalism Review's Meta Content Library analysis](https://www.cjr.org/tow_center/meta-is-getting-rid-of-crowdtangle.php). Related legal anchor: **Meta v. Bright Data** (N.D. Cal., Jan 23 2024), Judge Chen granting summary judgment for the scraper — a major precedent for public-data scraping and for researchers using scraping to *measure* platforms: [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2024/01/24/court-rules-in-favor-of-a-web-scraper-bright-data-which-meta-had-used-and-then-sued/). |
| **The DSA: Mandatory, Structured Transparency** | **Marquee current-event data.** The [DSA Transparency Database](https://transparency.dsa.ec.europa.eu/?lang=en) has crossed **~30 billion Statements of Reasons** cumulative since launch in Sept 2023 — 9.4 billion in one six-month window per the Commission's [January 2025 dashboard update](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/news/digital-services-act-commission-launches-transparency-database-update-dashboard). The critical finding for teaching: **~99%** of SoRs are platform-initiated (own terms of service), *not* legal orders — quantifying how little of online control is formal law. Critical assessment: [ITIF, Oct 2025 — EU Should Improve Transparency in the DSA](https://itif.org/publications/2025/10/20/eu-should-improve-transparency-in-the-digital-services-act/). Broader trend anchor: [Freedom House — Freedom on the Net 2025](https://freedomhouse.org/report/freedom-net) — **15th consecutive year of decline**; 70% of internet users live in countries where people were attacked or killed for online activity. |
| **Three Ways to Measure — Three Risk Profiles** | The cruel trade-off. Consented methods (researchers, volunteers) can't get continuous baseline coverage; co-opting methods can — but implicate uninvolved people. Book §5.5. |
| **Censorship Measurement Falls in a Gap** | IRBs review *human-subjects* research; measuring a censor is often not that on paper — but can still put people at risk. Legal ≠ safe. Book §5.5. |
| **The Paradox of Consent** | Book §5.5 "Paradox of Consent" — the most counterintuitive discussion moment. Consent may *increase* harm by creating documentary evidence of intent to test censorship; the noise of normal third-party traffic protects unwitting participants. Cite Encore's IRB determination (not human-subjects research under the Common Rule) — the ethical review moved to the SIGCOMM PC instead. |
| **Toward Ethical Norms** | Proportionality · transparency · harm minimization · community dialogue. Concrete harm-mitigation move: restrict reflectors to *infrastructure* machines (ISP resolvers, CDN nodes) — the standard defense in Ensafi et al. and Censored Planet's methodology. |
| **Key Takeaways** | Recap the three §5.1/§5.2/§5.5 boxes: honest bounded claims, multiple independent witnesses, ethical humility. |
| **Next: Taking Back Control** | Bridge to Ch. 6 (circumvention). Measurement and circumvention are two sides of the same coin — you measure to know what to circumvent, and OONI measures the blocking of Tor/Psiphon/VPNs. Point students at [OONI Explorer](https://explorer.ooni.org/) and the [DSA Transparency Database](https://transparency.dsa.ec.europa.eu/?lang=en) for project data. |
